Step Five

The house returned and became visible again. 
Neighbors would debate whether or not it was ever carried and if it weighed anything at all. 
Because - it might not contain actual mass, just a shredded past.

A group of people: 19, 23, 44, 68 moved in.
The walls were smoothed and antique-replica desks were placed in corners. 
The land they stood on was worthless, emptied. So was the outer shell of the house 
that has been subjected to treatment with layers of protection against the elements by ignorant residents. 
Inside, they used their skills to harvest decay and modify neurocircuitry: 
molding chipped wood with refired floor tile dust, filtering liquids in custom containers around foundations, 
and pipes into hyperconductive puddles. They formed new touch interfaces.

And their hard drives were removable. 
The enclosed impenetrable layers of their handhelds were cracked, scarred, and modular. 
They would go on experimental walks and exchange points of view, but would hardly talk. 
Their language became the pendulum of their arms handing off cued data: I have something to say; this is how I see it.

And then, Vera had a memory that wasn’t hers.
