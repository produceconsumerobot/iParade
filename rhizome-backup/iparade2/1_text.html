Step One

People with cameras are circling a tree.
Their eyes don’t squint.
Instead, they sway, arms swift, elbows bent, up and down.
Hands and fingers form an open half rectangle holding: silver, black,
pink, striped encased screens.
To choose an angle, they stagger. Once towards the lowered arm - head tilted, fingers cupping miniature-flattened realities. Rhythmic. 
Then, elbow up, they extend the space between the supporting arms and the captured texture.

The players align themselves with parallel visions that have been haunting.
The layer of expectations still hovers, the recorded energy of things-never-been.
